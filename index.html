Why These Matter in MCom

MCom is not just about writing more. It is about making communication useful. SMART helps shape the message. SIFT helps test the evidence behind it. Together, they create communication that is easier to understand and easier to trust.

1. Brief Overview

What are SMART and SIFT?

These two frameworks serve different purposes. One improves how a message is written. The other improves how information is evaluated.

SMART

A writing framework

SMART helps writers create communication that is focused, purposeful, and useful. In MCom, it is often used to shape agenda sentences and other task-oriented communication so the audience can quickly understand what is happening and why it matters.

SIFT

An evaluation framework

SIFT helps readers pause and examine information before trusting it. In MCom, it supports stronger research and better decision-making by helping students use reliable, well-supported sources instead of reacting too quickly to weak information.

2. Focus on SMART

SMART builds clear communication.

SMART is especially useful when the goal is to make writing more direct, practical, and audience-friendly.

What SMART is used for

SMART helps transform vague ideas into specific communication. Rather than writing something broad or unclear, a SMART sentence gives the audience a more precise sense of the task, decision, or discussion point.

  • S
    Specific
    Clearly identifies the topic or action.
  • M
    Measurable
    Points toward a concrete outcome or result.
  • A
    Action-oriented
    Focuses on what will be done, decided, or produced.
  • R
    Relevant
    Connects to the audience’s needs and the purpose of the message.
  • T
    Time-bound
    Shows when the task, decision, or discussion should happen.
Why it matters

Why SMART strengthens writing

It reduces vagueness.

Readers should not have to guess what a sentence means or what outcome is expected.

It improves professionalism.

Clear communication sounds more prepared, thoughtful, and useful in business and academic settings.

It respects the audience’s time.

When a message is focused, the audience can understand it more quickly and respond more effectively.

Weak Example

“Talk about improving social media.”

SMART Revision

“Decide on three social media strategies to increase student engagement during next month’s campaign.”

SMART checklist

  • Is the topic clear and focused?
  • Does the sentence point toward an outcome?
  • Does it describe a specific action or decision?
  • Is it relevant to the audience?
  • Does it include a timeframe when needed?
3. Focus on SIFT

SIFT strengthens credibility.

SIFT is less about writing the sentence and more about making sure the information inside the sentence deserves to be there.

What SIFT is used for

SIFT helps students slow down before accepting information as true. In a world full of fast claims, headlines, and posts, SIFT creates a habit of checking credibility before citing, repeating, or relying on information.

  • S
    Stop
    Pause before reacting to or using the information.
  • I
    Investigate the source
    Look at who created it, what they know, and whether they are credible.
  • F
    Find better coverage
    Compare with stronger, more established sources.
  • T
    Trace claims back
    Follow the information to the original study, report, or source when possible.
Why it matters

Why SIFT improves communication

It prevents misinformation.

Not every polished source is reliable. SIFT helps separate strong evidence from weak claims.

It supports stronger arguments.

Communication becomes more persuasive when it is built on dependable sources.

It builds trust.

Readers are more likely to believe a message when the evidence behind it is thoughtful and credible.

Without SIFT

Using the first article that appears in a search without checking the author, source, or evidence.

With SIFT

Checking the source, comparing coverage, and tracing the claim back to the original report before using it.

SIFT checklist

  • Did I pause before trusting the claim?
  • Do I know who created this information?
  • Have I compared it with better sources?
  • Can I trace the claim back to its original evidence?
4. Using Them Together

Strong communication needs both.

SMART and SIFT do different jobs, but they work best side by side.

SMART gives structure.

It helps a message become more focused, purposeful, and easier to act on. Without structure, ideas can feel vague or incomplete.

SIFT gives credibility.

It helps a writer examine evidence before relying on it. Without credibility, even well-written communication can be weak.

Before

“We should use online sources to improve our strategy.”

After SMART + SIFT

“Use two verified industry reports to identify three strategies for improving next month’s communication campaign.”

5. Final Takeaway

Clear communication is intentional.

The best messages are not only clear. They are also credible. That is why SMART and SIFT matter in MCom.

Remember this

Use SMART when you want your writing to be clear, focused, and useful. Use SIFT when you want your information to be trustworthy and well supported.

Together, they help writers communicate with greater confidence, stronger organization, and more credibility.
